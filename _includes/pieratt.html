A brief interview with...

BEN PIERATT
Ben Pieratt is a designer known for projects like Hessian and SVPPLY. He has been through the ringer as a startup CEO and came out the other side with quite a bit of personal drive and focus, which has lead to his most recent studio, Internetland.

In a short interview about the web, his professional career, "digital native", and chaos, he imparts some wisdom.
See also: the video interview and transcript.
"i'm inspired by the internet and i love the internet as a concept; this notion of world connection is something that as a designer i can feel good about contributing to"
"i can't draw. but in illustrator i feel very loose. so i'm trying to embrace digital, embrace the internet, embrace this embrace towards becoming an artist."
"books are going to be around for a long time and they'll be beautiful, but screens are going to get more interesting for a while..."
"...the thing that sold me is the retina display. it's so vibrant and so crisp that it contributes to digital artists embracing the screen as its own medium."
"it's fun to think about things native to the internet. i only work in RGB space; the colors i love most are the ones that can't exist in CMYK."
"i can put this magazine piece on a wall, even six years ago i wouldn't have thought it was beautiful. but now the patina of the shitty paper feels like something legitimate."
"print is dead so print is art."
"svpply was our first experiment, this notion of building design that takes chaos into account and then uses best practices to make something great."
"all of this design has to fail gracefully."
"that's really a problem when you're building a product and you don't like using it. you don't learn anything from it. it can't speak to you because you are not trying."
"don't be afraid of having style because it's a working exression of what components you find interesting in what you're working on."
"i love the internet. i love the web, but i don't like the app store because it's filtered through apple's editorial."
"if you want your thing to look new then make it one column and make it big. the moment you break something into two columns, it looks old."
"bring the internet into the slow-time; make it something you can appreciate as an art object."
